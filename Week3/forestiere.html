General Overview: Theory and Hypotheses

Social science research relies on theory to explain why things happen the way they do. A theory is a generalized explanation that connects causes and effects—essentially answering the question "why?"

To test these explanations, researchers create hypotheses—specific, testable statements about how two or more variables are related. For example, a theory might claim that education affects political participation, while a hypothesis might state, "People with more education are more likely to vote."
