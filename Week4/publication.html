Stage 2: Original Contribution

Improvement Strategy

  1. Improve presentation without changing assumptions
  2. Make minimal methodological changes
  3. Isolate and justify key changes
  4. Incorporate further improvements if beneficial

Stage 3: Validation & Finalization

Critical Question

"Whose mind are you going to change about what?"

If you can't answer this, your paper lacks contribution.

üìñ Key Terms & Definitions

Replication-First Strategy

A research approach that begins with replicating existing work before making original contributions, establishing credibility and reducing burden of proof.

Defensible Improvement

A methodological enhancement that can be clearly justified and demonstrated to strengthen the original research without fundamentally changing its core assumptions.

Burden of Proof

The responsibility to demonstrate that your research contribution is valid and meaningful. Reduced when building on replicated work.

Post-Treatment Bias

A methodological error where variables affected by the treatment are incorrectly used as controls, leading to biased estimates.

Alphabetical Authorship

A convention in social sciences where co-authors are listed alphabetically to signal equal contribution to the research.

Research Note

A shorter academic publication format (typically 10-20 pages) that presents focused findings or methodological improvements.

Uncertainty Measures

Statistical indicators (confidence intervals, standard errors) that quantify the precision of research estimates, essential for proper interpretation.

Cutting Edge Position

The advantage gained by successfully replicating recent work, positioning your research at the forefront of current knowledge in the field.

Internal Peer Review

The process of having classmates or colleagues review your work before submission, improving quality and building professional networks.

Active Voice

A writing style where the subject performs the action, preferred in academic writing for clarity and directness (e.g., "We analyzed" vs. "Analysis was conducted").
